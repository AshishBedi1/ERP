## 4. Probation

- **60-day** probation to assess suitability, fitness, and competency; review during this period; ongoing permanent employment confirmed thereafter if applicable.
- In the **first 60 days**, either party may terminate **for any reason**, with **one (1) day** prior notice (wording per source).
- Probation may **extend** based on performance.
- Probation is deemed from **date of joining**.
- **Two (2) paid leaves** allowed during probation.
- **One (1) short leave of two (2) hours** allowed **once per month** during probation.
- During probation, **no other benefits** (as per source).
- **Probation is not included** in appraisal timing (see performance section).

---

## 5. Attendance, punctuality & absence

- Be **regular and punctual**; if unable to attend, notify **immediate authority**.
- If absent due to **illness or accident**, notify **HR** so coverage can be arranged.
- If ill at work or leaving early, inform **HR**.
- **Absence without notice:** repeated absence without permission may be treated as **absconding** / contract breach (as per source).

### Lateness (from source)

- More than **2 hours** late → **half day** marked.  
- More than **4 hours** late → **full day absence** marked.

## 6. Annual leave & holidays (policy summary)

- **Five (5)** sick leave, **five (5)** casual leave, **eight (8)** earned leave, plus **public holidays** (sick/casual/earned **not** part of probation per source).
- Give **one (1) day** notice to HR where possible for scheduled leave; approval subject to coverage.
- Illness leave logged as **sick leave**; provide **doctor’s treatment letter** when applicable.
- **Eight (8) earned leaves** do **not** carry forward (per source).
- **No leaves** carry forward to the next calendar year; use in-year or **get paid for unused** (per source — confirm with HR).
- **Two (2) hour** short leave **once per month** (aligns with probation rule; confirm post-confirmation rules with HR).

**Notes:** Planned leave should be mutually agreed considering workload. Leave must be approved in advance except when absence cannot be anticipated. Leave records may be kept in the employee file.

**Public holidays:** See `../holidays/statutory-public-holidays.md`.

## 7. Maternity & paternity (summary)

- Maternity as per **Maternity Benefit Act** or **ESI**, whichever applies.
- Notify management at least **one (1) month** before expected leave start.
- May use accumulated annual leave before/after maternity as per policy.
- On return, employment in a **similar position** at same salary level (per source).
- **Miscarriage:** **six (6) weeks** leave from date of incident (per source).
- **Paternity (male employees):** **15 days** (earned leave) within **six (6) months** of delivery; inform employer **one week** in advance of probable birth; lapse if not taken within six months (per source).

## 9. Performance management

- Ongoing performance management; informal and formal reviews.
- **Appraisal:** after **6** calendar months if salary is **less than ₹15,000**; after **11** calendar months if salary is **₹15,000 or more** (per source).
- **Lock-in:** **3 months** after appraisal — cannot resign immediately after appraisal; otherwise may need to **return appraisal-related amounts** (per source — confirm with HR).

### Performance improvement

- Strive for improvement; company may assist.
- May include verbal/written warnings, counselling, retraining; unsuccessful improvement may lead to ending employment.
- Verbal warnings to be noted (date, HR sign); written warnings documented and copy to employee and file.

## 11. Termination of employment

### Resignation

- **First 60 days of probation:** **one (1) day** notice from employee or employer (per source).
- **After confirmation:** **30 days’** notice or wages in lieu, from **both** sides unless otherwise agreed in writing.
- Resignations **in writing only**.
- Resignation may not be accepted during paid absence/sick/leave **without director approval** (per source).
- Leave during notice **only with manager approval**; approved leave during notice **may not be paid** (per source).
- On termination, **return all company property**; relieving may be withheld until return (per source).

### Dismissal

- Serious misconduct only after **disciplinary procedure**; may be with or without notice depending on case (examples in source: theft/fraud, dishonesty, harassment, insubordination, etc.).
- Natural justice and fair hearing for disciplinary proceedings.

### Desertion

- Absent without permission **more than 3 consecutive days** may trigger desertion process: HR contact → final notice → possible termination; pay since last pay-day may be withheld; no service/relieving certificates; return during process may lead to disciplinary hearing (per source).

### Layoff

- Operational changes; efforts to retain similar role/salary; if not possible, **one (1) week** notice per year of service (per source).

### Termination without full & final

- Leaving **without notice** and not completing **full & final** may result in no salary/relieving/experience certificate and being marked **absconder**; may not re-apply (per source).

### Superannuation

- **Age 58** (per source).
